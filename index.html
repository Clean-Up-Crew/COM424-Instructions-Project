1) Read Instructions

Please be sure to read all instructions before using the humidifier.

2) Finding a Location

Finding a location for your humidifier is not hard, but a few conditions must be met first.

  • Find a place indoors. Place the device on an inside wall near an electrical outlet.
  • Make sure the humidifier is at least 4 in. (10 cm) away from the wall.
  • Keep it away from heating devices like stoves, radiators, and heaters.

A wall outlet in an indoor area. There's also plenty of space away from the wall for the humidifier.

  • Keep it out of reach of children, and keep it out of a closed room where a child may be sleeping, resting, or playing.
  • Do not use the humidifier in a room with 50% or greater humidity. Use a hygrometer, available at your local retailer or through the manufacturer, to measure humidity.
  • Make sure that the humidifier is not underneath any overhanging surfaces. Placement like this can damage the overhanging surface.

This dresser is a perfect place to keep the humidifier out of the reach of children.

3) How to Place Your Humidifier

Now that you have found a place for your humidifier, you need to set it up so that it will work as intended.

  • Wait for the unit to cool completely before handling it.
  • Place the humidifier on a firm, flat, level surface. A waterproof mat or pad is recommended.
  • NEVER place it on a rug or hardwood floor that can be damaged by moisture

Here's a flat surface away from the wall. It's perfect for the humidifier.

  • Face the humidifier away from the wall to prevent damage to the wall
  • NEVER place the humidifier under water flow or immerse in liquids

4) Plugging It In

The last step in this process is plugging in the humidifier. To minimize risk of fire or electrical shock, please remember the following:

  • Before plugging it in, make sure neither the cord nor device is damaged. Return it to manufacturer if so.
  • Be sure to plug the humidifier into a 120V AC electrical outlet. Plugging it into anything else may result in fire or electrical shock.
  • Do not plug in the cord with wet hands. Electrical shock could result.
  • Keep the cord out of heavy traffic areas. To reduce risk of fire, do not place the cord under a rug or near heat registers.
